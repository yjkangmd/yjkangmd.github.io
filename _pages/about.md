Young-Joon Kang, MD, PhD, is an Associate Professor at Incheon St. Mary's
Hospital, The Catholic University of Korea. He practices as a breast
surgical oncologist.

His research interests center on robotic breast surgery, artificial
intelligence and large language models in clinical oncology, and patient
quality of life. Current projects apply deep learning and large language
models to surgical decision support, treatment management, and clinical data
curation in breast cancer, and include prevention of chemotherapy-induced
peripheral neuropathy and management of treatment-related adverse events.
This work is supported by grants from the National Research Foundation of
Korea, the Institute for Bio-Medical Convergence, and the Korean Association
of Robotic Surgeons. His surgical research also addresses robotic
nipple-sparing mastectomy and axillary de-escalation, alongside
biomarker-based prognostication in breast cancer.

He is Deputy Editor of the Journal of Breast Cancer and a member of the Korea
Robot-Endoscopic Minimal Access Breast Surgery Study Group (KoREa-BSG), the
Korean Breast Cancer Study Group (KBCSG), and the Korea Oncoplastic Breast
Surgery Study Group (KOPBS).
